About me

Hi there!
I'm José Francisco Garita Sánchez, a Systems Engineering graduate from Universidad Fidélitas. I've always been drawn to the parts of a system nobody sees — the logic running quietly behind a button, holding everything else together. That's what pulled me into backend development: not the interface, but the plumbing that makes it trustworthy.

I like being the person a team can hand a messy, half-defined problem to and trust that it'll come back as something solid. I'm patient with complexity, stubborn about edge cases, and genuinely enjoy the moment a system finally clicks into place after hours of chasing down why it didn't. That combination of curiosity and follow-through is what I bring to every project I touch.

Password Manager

Personal Project

A desktop password manager built in Python. Stores every credential in an AES-encrypted local database, keeps the encryption key out of the codebase using environment variables, and covers the full CRUD lifecycle (add, view, update, delete) through a modern Tkinter GUI. Packaged as a standalone executable with PyInstaller, so it runs on Windows without requiring Python to be installed — the kind of security-first design informed by my Ethical Hacking and Cybersecurity certifications.
